Water pollution is a great problem in the Chesapeake Bay
caused by excess nutrients in the bay. Fairfax County's water is
also affected by pollution with its main cause being polluted
storm water runoff.
Saving Our Water
Learn more about stopping and preventing water pollution
It may not seem like it, but even small actions can
help clean our watersheds and the bay. To help:
Limit the uses of pesticides/fertilizer
Cover bare soil (prevent erosion)
Do not litter
Volunteer in clean up programs
Pick up your animals waste
Compost/Mulch leaves and grass clippings
To Prevent Pollution:
Use low flow faucets/showerheads/toilets
Take short showers
Turn of the tap when not in use
Run your washing machine and dishwasher only when full
Avoid using hot water in the washing machine/dishwasher
Use a drip irrigation system for plants
Water plants early in the morning or in the evening to prevent evaporation
Use brooms instead of a hose to clean driveways, walkways, and decks.
